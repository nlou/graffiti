Cleaning graffiti in Midtown is more than twice as slow as in the Lower East Side

Not all graffiti is treated equal.

It turns out that walking in Times Square is not only a drag, but the throngs of tourists and wax figure enthusiasts are making it hard to clean graffiti. In a year’s worth of data between February 2014 and February 2015, a reported case of graffiti took an average of 136 days to clean in Midtown South, or Police Precinct 14. Compare that to a zippy 55 days in the Lower East Side, and 70 days in Staten Island. Here’s something else you can blame on tourists.
